# Something a little different this [unit/quarter/semester]

Hey [class name],

This [unit/quarter/semester] we're trying something I want you to hear about straight from me.

## The short version

For the next [duration], I'm using an AI assistant to help me do the behind-the-scenes work of teaching this class. It helps me prepare slides, encouragement cards, parent updates, and weekly reflections.

**You will never talk to the AI directly.** I am always the bridge. The AI doesn't see your name, your grades, or anything personal about you. It only sees broad summaries of how the class is doing — for example, "12 students in Period 3 are caught up on assignments this week" — never anything tied to a specific person.

## What you'll notice

- Probably better-looking handouts than I usually produce
- More frequent notes from me (usually good ones — celebrations and check-ins, not just "you owe work")
- Themed slides that connect to whatever we're working on
- Occasional certificates if you hit a milestone

## What's the AI's name?

[If decided already: **[AI NAME]**. Brief one-line description of who they are.]

[If students will help decide: We're going to vote on it together. I'll explain that in class.]

## What this is NOT

- It's not a chatbot for you to talk to.
- It's not grading anything.
- It's not deciding anything about you.
- It's not watching you or tracking your behavior outside of normal gradebook stuff that I already had.

## What you can do

- **Ask me anything about it.** If you're curious, suspicious, or just have a question, ask. I'd rather you ask than wonder.
- **Tell me when something doesn't land.** If a handout is weird, a slide is off, or a message I send your family sounds wrong — tell me. Your feedback makes the AI better.
- **Opt out if you want to.** If you don't want any AI-touched materials coming your way, tell me (or have a family member tell me). You'll get the same content I'd have made without the AI. No big deal, no questions asked, nothing different about how I treat you in class.

## A note from me

I'm honestly not sure how this will go. That's part of why I'm telling you. This is a real experiment, and you're not the experiment — you're the people I'm trying to teach better. The AI is just a tool I'm trying out.

If at any point it feels like the AI is in the way instead of helping, we'll change course.

Thanks for trying this with me.

[Your name]

## (Optional) Frequently asked questions

You can add an FAQ section if your students tend to be question-askers. A few good starter Q&As:

> **Q: So the AI is grading me?**
> A: No. I grade everything. The AI never sees your grade or assigns one.

> **Q: Can the AI read my messages or essays?**
> A: No. The AI doesn't see anything you write unless I specifically choose to share *themes* from an anonymous class-wide survey — and even then, the AI only sees patterns across the whole class, not anything tied to you.

> **Q: Why are you doing this?**
> A: [Your honest reason. One or two sentences. Examples: "I want to be better at noticing when someone is quietly falling behind." / "I want to communicate with your families more often, and the AI helps me do that without it taking over my evenings." / "I'm curious whether this kind of tool can make me a more present teacher."]

> **Q: What if I don't want to be part of it?**
> A: Tell me or have a family member tell me. You'll get the same content without the AI involved. There's no penalty and nothing changes about how I treat you.

> **Q: Could my parents see this?**
> A: Yes — I sent them a letter explaining the whole thing. They can opt you out at any time.

> **Q: Will you tell us how it's going?**
> A: Yes. At the end of the [unit/quarter], we'll talk about what worked and what didn't. I'll be honest.
