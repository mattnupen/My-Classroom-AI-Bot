# Letter to Families — Template

*Send this home in the first week of the experiment. Adapt the language to your school's tone. Send it via your usual channel (ParentSquare, email, paper). Always send it after you've gotten admin approval.*

---

**Subject:** A note about a small experiment in [class name]

Dear families,

I'm writing to let you know about something a little different we're trying in [class name] this [quarter / semester]. I want you to hear it from me before your student does.

## What we're trying

For the next [duration], I'll be using an AI assistant — named [AI name, if you have one, or "to be named by the students themselves"] — to help me with the behind-the-scenes work of teaching. The AI helps me prepare:

- Slides for class
- Encouragement cards I hand to students
- Updates to send home to families (like this one)
- Celebration certificates for students who hit milestones
- Reflections on how the class is going week by week

## What this does NOT mean

- **Your student is not talking to an AI.** I am always the bridge. No AI ever talks to your child or grades their work.
- **Your student's personal data is not being shared with the AI.** Grades and individual student details are handled by small browser tools that run only on my laptop. The AI only sees broad class summaries, never anything tied to your student's name.
- **I am not replaced or automated.** Every piece of content the AI produces, I review before it reaches your student or you. The AI saves me time on logistics — it doesn't replace teaching.

## Why I'm doing this

[Your honest reason, one or two sentences. Examples:
- "I want to spend less time on paperwork and more time on the human parts of teaching."
- "I want to communicate with families more often than I currently manage to."
- "I'm hoping this helps me catch students who are quietly falling behind, before it gets bigger."]

## What your student will notice

- Occasional handouts (printed cards or progress sheets) that look more designed than what I usually produce
- Possibly themed weekly slides or posters
- More frequent notes home (good ones — celebrations and check-ins, not just bad news)

## What you can do

- **Ask your student about it.** They'll know what's going on. If anything they describe concerns you, please reach out.
- **Reply to any messages you get from me.** If a message about your student doesn't sound right to you — wrong tone, missing context — tell me, and I'll adjust.
- **Opt out if you want to.** If you'd rather your student not be part of this, reply to this message and they'll receive the same materials I would have produced without the AI. There's no penalty and no special treatment either way.

## Questions

Email me at [your email] or reply to this message. I'm happy to walk through exactly how this works, what the AI does and doesn't do, or to show you the materials before they go out.

Thank you for trusting me with your kid. I take this seriously.

[Your name]
[Your title]
[Your school]

## A note for the teacher (delete before sending)

A few practical things:

- **Keep it short.** Many families will only read the first paragraph. The most important sentences are in the first three paragraphs.
- **Don't oversell.** Don't promise outcomes. The honest framing — "I'm trying this; here's what it is; you can opt out" — lands better than enthusiasm.
- **Be ready for questions.** Some families will email. Have your privacy explainer (`privacy-explainer.md`) ready to forward.
- **Don't forward family responses to the AI.** If a parent writes you with a question or concern, that's between you and them.
- **If a family opts out:** keep a quiet list of opted-out students. When you run the local tools, filter them out or generate their materials manually. The point is that they shouldn't notice they're being treated differently — they just don't get AI-touched content.
